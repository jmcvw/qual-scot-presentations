##  Task 1:

In January 2026, we published our __Progression Official Statistics in Development__ publication, (<https://www.sqa.org.uk/sqa/105163.html>) along with an accompanying interactive app.

You should review the publication's __background information__, statistical summary, tables and __interactive app__.
Your task is to identify __'two stars and two wishes'__.
i.e. two elements or aspects of the publication that you consider are __worthy of positive recognition__, and two where __further development__ could be made.

You will have a maximum of __ten minutes__ to talk through the four items that you have identified, __giving reasons__ for your choices. 


##  Task 2:

Every year we publish summary tables of statistics for the Skills for Work, Awards, National Progression Awards and National Certificates qualifications.
These are included in tables in many of the 'Attainment Statistics 2025' Excel workbooks that are downloadable from here: <https://www.sqa.org.uk/sqa/48269.8311.html>.

Your task is to provide a __high level analysis__ of at least one of these __qualification types__ using the available data .

The focus of the analysis can be on any aspect of the data of your choosing.
__Visualisations__ of the data should form a __key part__ of your presentation to aid the discussion of your findings, as well as the potential implications for future years.

You will have a maximum of __ten minutes__ to share the findings and discussions from your analysis.

We also would like to __have sight of the code__ that you used for this task.
Please send by email both your __slides and your code__, to <peopleresourcing@qualifications.gov.scot> at least __24 hours__ before your interview time.
For the slides, we can accept any standard file type.
For the code, we can accept any R or Python code file(s) that can be opened with RStudio or Visual Studio Code.
Alternatively, we also welcome a hyperlink to an online repository of your code, such as GitHub.

On interview day, your presentation resources will have been pre-loaded onto a Windows laptop, that is connected to a large screen display, along with a wired mouse and a wireless presentation clicker.
